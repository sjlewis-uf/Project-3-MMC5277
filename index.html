Park City Mountain

Park City is it's own ski town with the largest lift-served terrain in the U.S. and features two separate bases: Park City Mountain Village and Canyons Village. There are more than 330 ski runs across 17 peaks with 41 lifts accomodating the often busy resort. Canyons is the larger of the two areas with a good mix of terrain; on-hill restaurants; and a bustling base area. Park City Mountain Village excells in intermediate runs with space for novice skiers.

With the town of Park City at it's base, Park City's Main Street offers great restaurants and hotels. It is also host to the annual Sundance Film Festival.

Park City Mountain is included with the EPIC pass.

Snowbird

Snowbird is a powder-hound's delight with amazing intermediate and expert terrain. Known for fresh snow and rugged terrain, Snowbird has a season that can extend into the early summer months! Giving Snowbird one of the most efficient lift systems is a 125-passenger tram that provides access to almost all areas of the mountain. Unique to this resort is a tunnel through the mountain with a conveyr belt providing a "ski-through" mining museum experience.

Snowbird is connected to the adjacent Alta Ski Resort (skiers only) at the top of Sugarloaf Pass. However, with no real ski town at it's base, there are fewer hospitality options compared to Park City.

Snowbird is part of the IKON and Mountain Collective pass programs.

Brighton

Brighton shines the value it brings to skiers and snowboarders looking for quality time on the runs. As one of the oldest ski areas in the U.S., it is Utah's first and provides a reliable, less crowded option compared to others in the area. Brighton has the advantage of some of the most accessbile terrain through its high-speed quads in addition to its magic carpet and a triple. With a variety of terrain, this resort is a solid choice for all levels of skiers and boarders.

Brighton offers night skiing every night, except Sundays, from December to March and children under 10 ski free! There are few food and lodging options so most choose to stay in nearby Salt Lake City and commute to the resort during the day.

Brighton is connected to the nearby Solitude Mountain resort by the SolBright Trail with SolBright tickets providing access to both resorts. Brighton is also part of the IKON pass program.

Skiers Only Resorts

Deer Valley

Deer Valley prides itself on customer service and providing a luxuray ski experience from it's runs to the dining and lodging facilities. This skiers only resort features expansive groomed runs and a superb skiing school. Deer Valley is also one of the few resorts with a licensed child care facility for non-skiing children. Luxory accomodations will cost more than other resorts though.

Keeping with the luxory feel, Deer Valley limits the number of skiers they allow each day so their slopes are never too crowded. Deer Valley does participate in the IKON pass program.

Alta Ski

Alta is a skiers only option for old-school enthusiasts looking for extreme terrain but also offers one of the best ski schools in the country with solid beginner's terrain. The six main lifts make Alta an easy resort to navigate but there are limited amenities for food and lodging. The main base is the Wildcat with the most facilities and the ski school is located at the second base, Albion.

Alta neighbors the Snowbird resort and a combined lift ticket provides access to both. Like Snowbird, Alta is part of the IKON and Mountain Collective pass programs.
